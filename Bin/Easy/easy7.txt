Tihar, also known as Deepawali and Yamapanchak or Swanti is a five-day-long Hindu festival celebrated in Nepal and in the Indian states of Assam and Sikkim including in Darjeeling district of West Bengal. It is the festival of lights, as diyas are lit inside and outside the houses to make it illuminate at night. It is popularly known as Swanti among the Newars and as Deepawali among Madhesis.
Set in the Vikram Samvat calendar, the festival begins with
 Kaag Tihar in Trayodashi of Kartik Krishna Paksha and ends
 with Bhai Tika in Dwitiya of Kartik Sukla Paksha every year.
